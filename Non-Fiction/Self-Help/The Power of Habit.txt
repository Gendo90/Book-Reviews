Author: Charles Duhigg
Description: This book provides a great background on the fundamentals of behavior change, especially with respect to habits. It examines how habits are formed, how they can be changed, and how some habits can be connected to other habits. There are examples and evidence from scientific research, with short vignettes demonstrating the scientific principles behind habit formation and change at the individual, organizational, and societal level. This book can easily be used as a guide to stop bad habits like smoking, overeating, or drinking, and/or to develop new habits like exercise and consistent improvement of work skills. It can be used to help individuals, organizations, or communities grow in the right direction by enabling the right habits to be formed to achieve the desired results, one step at a time.
